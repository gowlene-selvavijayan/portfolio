About Me

Gowlene Selvavijayan (they/she) is a neuroqueer Ottawa-based journalist, community organizer, digital communications specialist and storyteller. Born and raised in Toronto, they moved to Ottawa in 2015 to study journalism and human rights at Carleton University. As a J-School graduate and communications professional of over eight years, they are no stranger to working with multiple overlapping priorities in high-pressure environments with quick turnarounds.

Gowlene is committed to the ongoing learning, practicing and embedding of anti-racism, equity and accessibility in web design and development, communications, community programming and the organizations they work with. In their work within academia, non-profits and the public service and as a freelancer, they have worked to strengthen the impact of research, academic scholarship, and community-based knowledge and make data, information and stories more accessible, compelling, practical, and understandable. Their work and strategic advice have supported organizations in fostering safety, community, knowledge sharing, and greater access to resources, especially for racialized, queer and trans, disabled and neurodivergent youth and adults.

Gowlene takes pride in their curiosity, creativity, and ability to quickly learn new skills and technology to see a project to its utmost potential and streamline processes. Now, through Seneca's Web Design and Development Certificate Program, they hope to gain the foundational knowledge, practical skills and other tools they need to support organizational growth and transformation and thrive as a web developer.
