Who We Are

Spectrum Sensory Therapy is an Ottawa-based pediatric sensory therapy clinic and hub for children and youth, ages 0 to 18.

We are a multidisciplinary team of occupational therapists, speech-language pathologists, dieticians, psychologists, physiotherapists as well as arts-based and somatic therapists.

We take a holistic, collaborative approach, working closely with caregivers, schools and other professionals to build a robust support system to help children thrive.

Neurodiversity informs the different ways people experience the world and is not a deficit that needs to be labelled or "fixed". Our clinic is committed to providing affirming care to neurodivergent and disabled kids and supporting them in learning about themselves, feeling confident, and leading happy and fulfilled lives.

Our Mission

At Spectrum Sensory Therapy, we are dedicated to our holistic, collaborative, community-based approach to curate a strong support network to ensure children in our care are equipped to better identify, understand and address their needs and feel empowered to reach their goals. Our mission is to enable kids to maximize their independence and have meaningful experiences, while providing services rooted in the following values:

  • Compassion

    We approach every child and client with empathy and an openness to understand of our varied lived experiences. We know that every child deserves a safe, nurturing and affirming space to make them feel understood, supported and empowered.

  • Equity

    We actively oppose and work to address racism, systemic oppression and inequity in all its forms and are committed to ensuring our services and supports feel safe, welcoming and valuable to all. We are dedicated to continually educating ourselves, challenging biases, and advocating for anti-racism and equity, so that every child feels valued and supported within our care.

  • Accessibility

    We are committed to fostering an environment where every child has equal access to high-quality sensory therapy services and supports, regardless of their background, abilities, or socio-economic status. Our clinic is dedicated to breaking down barriers to access by providing affordable and flexible, virtual and in-person services. Please get in touch if you'd like more information on our sliding scale prices, or if you'd like to pay it forward.

  • Community

    We believe in the power of collaboration with families, schools and the broader community. By prioritizing community building, we strive to foster supportive, sustainable care networks that equip and empower children to learn more about their own needs and step further into their best selves.

  • Learning

    We embrace continuous learning. Our commitment to ongoing learning and innovative community-based practices ensures that we continue to expand our knowledge to provide the most effective resources, strategies and supports that resonate with the children we serve.

What We Offer

In Clinic

Visit us at our Centretown location for one-on-one and group workshops, and check out our sensory room while you wait!

At School

Calling all educators and school administrators! From offering trainings to learning assessments to designing sensory-friendly learning environments, we are here to make your classrooms more accessible and engaging for every student. Reach out to us for a consultation.

At Home

Can't come in person? We'll bring our services to you, in the comfort of your home.

Online

We also offer virtual one-on-one assessments, appointments and group sessions through our secure telehealth video platform.

Groups

We offer various groups for kids of all ages, from art therapy to social skills to emotional regulation to self-esteem. We also offer closed groups exclusively for 2SLGBTQ+ kids.

Get in Touch

Our Space

Come visit our vibrant space, where growth and development meets play. Our state-of-the-art sensory gym engages kids with dynamic, interactive elements and encourages sensory play. Every corner of our clinic is designed to spark curiousity and inspire growth.

As a community hub, we strive to ensure children and their families feel supported and apart of our community. We often organize community events and workshops in our space.
